Unlike technical exploits, social engineering targets **human psychology** rather than software vulnerabilities, making it uniquely difficult to defend against through technical means alone.

## Report Highlights

### Attack Types Covered
- **Phishing** — Mass deceptive email campaigns
- **Spear Phishing** — Targeted, OSINT-driven phishing
- **Whaling** — Executive-targeted attacks (CEO Fraud)
- **Vishing** — Voice/phone-based social engineering
- **Smishing** — SMS-based phishing
- **Pretexting** — Fabricated identity/scenario attacks
- **Baiting** — Physical (USB drops) and digital lures
- **Quid Pro Quo** — Fake IT support attacks
- **Tailgating / Piggybacking** — Physical access exploitation
- **Watering Hole Attacks** — Compromising trusted websites
- **Business Email Compromise (BEC)** — Invoice and wire transfer fraud
- **Honey Traps** — Fake relationship-based espionage

### Prevention Framework
- Security Awareness Training (phishing simulations, role-specific training, vishing drills)
- Technical Controls (DMARC/SPF/DKIM, FIDO2 MFA, DNS filtering, EDR, Zero Trust)
- Process Controls (wire transfer dual-authorization, helpdesk identity verification, vendor segmentation)
- Incident Response playbook for social engineering events
- Emerging Threat Analysis: **AI-powered attacks**, deepfake voice/video, LLM-generated phishing
